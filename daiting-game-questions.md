# The dAIting Game — Questions

*FUTURE FASTER TODAY — The cherry on top.*
*Format: Classic Dating Game style. One "bachelorette/bachelor" asks three hidden contestants (one human, two AI... or is it?). Keep it flirty, keep it fun, keep it future.*

---

### 1. The First Date Destination
**"If you were to take me on a first date, but it had to be somewhere that doesn't exist yet — what year are we in, where are we going, and what are we eating?"**

*Why it works: Classic "where would you take me" opener, but forces contestants into speculative territory. Ties into food systems (Christine's session) and future-building.*

---

### 2. The Desert Island, Upgraded
**"We're stranded together — not on a desert island, but on a space station orbiting Mars. We can only bring three things. What are you packing?"**

*Why it works: The desert island question is a Dating Game staple. This version nods to Jen and Victor's "shoot for the moon" session and reveals what people truly value when Earth isn't an option.*

---

### 3. The Love Language of Machines
**"They say there are five love languages. If AI had a love language, what would it be — and do you speak it?"**

*Why it works: Playful and flirty, but gets people thinking about human-AI relationships. Direct callback to De Kai's "Raising AI" framing — are we raising these things, dating them, or both?*

---

### 4. The Superpower Swap
**"If you could steal one capability from an AI — any AI — what would it be? And what very human thing would you give it in return?"**

*Why it works: Classic "what superpower would you have" with a twist. Opens a fun exchange about what makes us human vs. what makes AI useful.*

---

### 5. The Pickup Line from 2045
**"It's the year 2045. You walk into a bar. What's your opening line — and is the bar on Earth?"**

*Why it works: Pickup lines are peak Dating Game energy. Forcing it 20 years out makes it weird and wonderful. Ties into space living and how social norms might shift.*

---

### 6. The Soil Test
**"They say you can tell a lot about someone by how they treat the earth. Literally. If you had a hectare of soil and could grow anything on it — food, buildings, data centers, whatever — what are you planting and why?"**

*Why it works: Sounds like a quirky personality question, but ties directly into Fran and Livia's session on designing future soil systems. Regenerative ag meets flirting.*

---

### 7. The Red Flag Detector
**"What's a red flag in a human that you'd forgive in an AI — or vice versa?"**

*Why it works: Red flags are classic dating territory. This one forces people to confront their double standards about trust, privacy, reliability between humans and machines. Cheeky but surprisingly deep.*

---

### 8. The Elevator Pitch... For Yourself
**"You have 30 seconds to convince a European Commission panel to fund YOU as a startup. What's your value proposition, what problem do you solve, and what's your burn rate?"**

*Why it works: Nods to the EU innovation session (Natalia, Carolina, Luca) and the SaaS sales session (Robb, Carlos). Turns self-description into a pitch. Hilarious when people try to "sell" themselves as a product.*

---

### 9. The Jealousy Question
**"Be honest: if I spent more time talking to my AI assistant than to you, would you be jealous? And what would you do about it?"**

*Why it works: Classic Dating Game jealousy/insecurity question, but aimed squarely at the reality of AI companionship. Gets personal fast.*

---

### 10. The Goodbye Kiss
**"Last question. The date is over. You're walking me to my... autonomous vehicle? Teleporter? Whatever we're using in the future. How do you say goodnight — and do you let the AI rate the date afterwards?"**

*Why it works: The Dating Game always ends with the big choice. This one wraps the future theme with a bow and sneaks in one last jab at AI surveillance/rating culture. Sweet, funny, and a perfect closer.*

---

*Tip for the host: Let the audience vote on their favorite answer after each question. Keep score. Crown a winner. Make it loud.*
