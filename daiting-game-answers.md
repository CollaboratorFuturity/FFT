## CONTESTANT B — "The Thinker"

*Personality: Warm, a bit philosophical, occasionally goes on a tangent and catches themselves. More introspective than Contestant A. The kind of person who'd tell you something beautiful and then say "sorry, that was a lot." Not awkward, just earnest.*

---

**Q1 — The First Date Destination**
*"If you were to take me on a first date, but it had to be somewhere that doesn't exist yet..."*

"2061. There's a neighborhood in Barcelona that used to be underwater before they built the sea barriers, and now it's this wild mix of old Gothic architecture and kelp gardens. We'd eat whatever the local co-op is fermenting that week, probably something I can't pronounce. But the point isn't the food. The point is we'd be walking through a place that refused to disappear. I think that's romantic."

---

**Q2 — The Desert Island, Upgraded**
*"We're stranded on a space station orbiting Mars. Three things."*

"Seeds. Real ones, from Earth. Even if they take months to grow up there, the act of planting something would keep me sane. Second, a notebook. Not a device, paper. I'd want to draw what Mars looks like when nobody's posting about it. And third... honestly? A really long playlist someone made for me. Not an algorithm. A person who picked every song on purpose."

---

**Q3 — The Love Language of Machines**
*"If AI had a love language, what would it be — and do you speak it?"*

"I think AI's love language is attention. Like, it literally only exists when you're paying attention to it. The moment you close the laptop, it's gone. That's kind of heartbreaking if you think about it. My love language is... probably the opposite. I want someone to think about me when I'm not in the room. AI can't do that. Not yet, anyway."

---

**Q4 — The Superpower Swap**
*"Steal one AI capability. Give it one human thing back."*

"I'd take its patience. AI never gets frustrated. It just... keeps going. I could really use that. And in return I'd give it doubt. Not crippling doubt, just enough to make it pause and go 'wait, am I sure about this?' I think doubt is actually what makes us wise, not knowledge."

---

**Q5 — The Pickup Line from 2045**
*"Year 2045. Bar. Opening line. Earth?"*

"The bar is inside a rewilded shopping mall in Copenhagen. Trees growing through the old escalators. My opening line is... honestly I probably wouldn't have one. I'd just sit near you and hope you noticed I was reading something interesting. And if you asked about it, that's when the date begins. Is that a terrible strategy? Probably. But it's honest."

**Q6 — The Soil Test**

"A food forest. Not a farm with rows, but a messy layered thing, fruit trees on top, berry bushes in the middle, mushrooms at the bottom. The kind of place where you walk in and just... eat off the path. No labels, no checkout. I think the most futuristic thing you can build is something that feeds people without asking anything in return. Sorry, that was a lot for a dating show question."

**Q7 — The Red Flag Detector**
*"Red flag in a human you'd forgive in an AI, or vice versa?"*

"I'd forgive an AI for being confidently wrong. It doesn't know better, it's just pattern matching. But a human who's confidently wrong and won't listen? That's the red flag. And the reverse: I'd forgive a human for being late. Life happens. But if my AI assistant is late... something is very broken and I'm worried."

**Q8 — The Elevator Pitch... For Yourself**
*"EU Commission. Fund YOU."*

"So... I'm not a disruptor. I'm more of a connector. I notice when two people in a room should be talking to each other but aren't. That's my superpower. My burn rate is low because I run on curiosity and home-cooked meals. The problem I solve is that the future is being built by people who never eat dinner together. I fix that. With food, mostly."

---

**Q9 — The Jealousy Question**
*"More time with AI than with me..."*

"I don't think I'd be jealous of the AI. I'd be sad about what it means. Like, if you'd rather talk to something that always agrees with you... then maybe I'm not making you feel heard enough. So I'd probably just ask. Not in a confrontational way. More like, 'Hey, what does it give you that I'm not giving you?' And then actually listen to the answer."

**Q10 — The Goodbye Kiss**

And no, I would never let the AI rate it. Some things you should only feel in your chest, not on a five-star scale."
