Day 1 — Thursday, Feb 26 8 sessions
10:00
Food fight? Collaboration for food startups & corporates
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  Christine Gould
FAST
Lead: Christine Gould (Thought for Food / GIGA)
Corporate innovation goals often align with — and depend on — agile startups paving the way. But timelines, scope, and scale are often out of whack, especially in massive, complex industries like food and agriculture. Learn how companies, investors, startups, and governments can collaborate to take proven solutions and get them adopted — at speed and at system level.
10:45
Beyond regeneration: Designing future soil systems
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  Fran Antequera, Livia Kalossaka
FAST
Leads: Fran Antequera (AgriTech Center) & Livia Kalossaka (CDL)
Regenerative agriculture set the direction, but what comes next? This session pushes beyond current frameworks to explore how we might actively design soil ecosystems for resilience, governance, and territorial stability — repositioning soil as living, programmable, and strategic infrastructure for a climate-constrained future.
12:00
AI and Sustainable Mobility
📍 Track B – Business  ·  Núria Albó Canals
FAST
Lead: Núria Albó Canals (Min. de Transportes y Movilidad Sostenible)
How will AI, autonomous vehicles, and quantum computing change urban and global logistics? AI engineer and data scientist Núria Albó will talk about the Spanish Ministry of Digital Transformation's AI initiatives in mobility and sustainability.
12:45
Building an EU-first innovation ecosystem
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  Natalia Olsen, Carolina Angelini, Luca Cominassi
FAST
Leads: Natalia Olsen (CataValley), Carolina Angelini (Barcelona & Partners), Luca Cominassi (Metagov/OECD)
Geopolitics are shifting faster, and in more unexpected ways, than even seasoned strategists expected. In this age of uncertainty, European states, cities, innovation clusters, investors, and startups are all rethinking their futures — and finding common cause together.
14:30
AI's Next Move
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  De Kai
FAST
Lead: De Kai (HKUST)
We think of AI as a superintelligence, but what if it's more like a child — that needs to be raised (by us!) to learn hygiene, manners, kindness alongside knowledge. Discuss with noted professor and AI developer De Kai, author of "Raising AI".
15:15
Selling AI SaaS – Software as a Service to Services as a Software
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  Robb Miller, Carlos Grau
Facilitator-Led
Leads: Robb Miller (Fractional) & Carlos Grau (Grau Innovation)
AI adoption is a scramble, but it's quickly entering enterprise offerings, which will shift how it's sold. Robb Miller, a global SaaS sales thought leader, will talk about cross-border lessons on pipeline generation, consumption pricing, and trading margin for moat. In conversation with Carlos Grau, they will discuss "Service as a Software" — a new kind of SaaS disrupting professional services.
16:30
How to shoot for the moon – and save the Earth
📍 Track C – Planet  ·  Jen Stein, Victor Luo
Facilitator-Led
Leads: Jen Stein, PhD (Aerospace Corp.) & Victor Luo (ETH Zürich)
Building on her Stanford d.school class with the participation of the SETI Institute, Blue Origin, and Google X, Miki Sode will guide participants through how space innovation and systems thinking can unlock bold solutions here on the ground. From lunar missions to regenerative design — this talk is about aiming high, with both feet planted in planetary purpose.
